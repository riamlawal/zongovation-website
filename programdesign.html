PROGRAMS
Zongo Coders Challenge

The Zongo Coders Challenge provide young tech developers from across the
Zongo communities the opportunity to showcase their projects and ideas to
investors and partners for support and funding. The Zongo Coders Challenge
is opened to every young developer from any of the Zongo communities in Ghana.
ongoVation Hub’s approach is different – we don’t directly take equity,
invest in or impose success metrics or membership time limits on our startups.
However, we connect them with venture capital funds and investors.
Zongo Women in Tech

The main objective of Zongo Women in Tech is to encourage
more women from the Zongo communities to become tech
enthusiasts and entrepreneurs. Our members benefit from the flexibility and freedom to build their
businesses and develop their products how and when they want, with
the consistent support of our dedicated teams and a community of like-minded peers. We work exclusively with young entrepreneurs, not least tech entrepreneurs, by
understanding the process they go through and the needs they have at each stage
of their entrepreneurial journey. We support startups across all stages of their
development – from ideation to exit.

Zongo Entrepreneurship Forum

The Zongo Entrepreneurship Forum is a gathering of young entrepreneurs
from across the Zongo communities to discuss issues related to technology,
innovation, and entrepreneurship. During the event, participants discuss practical
solutions on how to address the socio-economic and environmental challenges
facing the Zongo communities in Ghana using technology, innovation and entrepreneurship.The main goal of this project is bridging the skills
gap between academia and industry by inspiring student interest in Analytics,
Financial Modeling & Microsoft Excel.
One-Million Young Entrepreneurs Program

One of the flagship programs at the ZongoVation Hub is the
One-Million Young Entrepreneurs Program, a program dedicated
to the training and equipping of one million young people from across
deprived and marginalized communities in Ghana to become entrepreneurs
...building startups to solve problems and create jobs for other young people in Ghana.



Zongo Coders Program

The Zongo Coders Program trains and mentors young people in technology creation,
entrepreneurship, and leadership skills to transform their lives and communities.
The vision of the Zongo Coders Program is to turn the Zongo communities in Ghana
into the tech hub of Africa by 2030 in line with the UN Sustainable Development Goals.
Visit www.zongocoders.org for details. The ZongoVation Hub will provide new perspectives.
Whether working in the co-working space, attending event or training workshops, or
receiving in house consulting services, the Hub will create an environment that fosters
innovation and ideation. The Hub will spark the entrepreneurial culture of the Zongo
communities in Ghana. It will help members and young people to develop creative habits
that will place them ahead of the current, ensuring they are leading the competition.


The One-Million Young Entrepreneurs Program is line with Goal 8 of the UN Sustainable Development Goal
of providing decent jobs for young people by the year 2030. The goal of this program is to introduce
to young people from deprived communities financial literacy and entrepreneurship. The program
will offer practical training in entrepreneurship to young people across all 16 regions of Ghana. The Excel Our Girls project is a fully sponsored, hands-on training program for talented
undergraduate and graduate girls. The objective of the project is to train girls to build
data-driven solutions using Microsoft Excel and any other programming language
(Python, R, Power BI, Power Apps etc.).
